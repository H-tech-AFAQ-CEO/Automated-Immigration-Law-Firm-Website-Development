Common Questions

Everything you need to know

Your engagement is formally initiated after your free 15-minute consultation call with your attorney. During this call, the attorney confirms that your documents are adequate to proceed. Payment is then collected by our office to activate the matter.

Government (USCIS) filing fees are separate from our flat attorney fees. They vary by petition type and are paid directly to USCIS. We'll provide you with a complete breakdown of all applicable filing fees during your consultation call so there are no surprises.

Once our office has received all required documents from you, we guarantee your matter will be filed within 14 calendar days. The clock starts when we have everything we need — not when you first contact us. If you need faster processing, our expedited option ($500 add-on) moves your matter to priority status.

You provide payment information during the intake process. However, your payment is processed manually by our office — only after your free 15-minute consultation confirms the adequacy of your documents and formally initiates your engagement. We handle all payment collection; no payments are taken automatically through the website.

Each separate I-130 petition is $500 in addition to your primary package. There is no limit to how many you can add. All petitions are covered by the same 2-week filing guarantee and are handled by the same attorney overseeing your case.

Docketwise is the leading immigration-specific case management platform, trusted by over 10,000 immigration firms. It uses 256-bit encryption for all data transmission and storage. Your intake questionnaire, documents, fee agreement, and case communication all live securely in your private client portal — accessible only by you and your attorney.

No attorney can guarantee USCIS approval, as outcomes depend on facts, evidence, and government discretion. We guarantee our process: thorough preparation, timely filing, and attorney review of every matter. Our flat-fee structure aligns our interests with yours — we succeed when your case is well-prepared.
